TikTok's 631 enforcement removals come from two data sources with different timestamp precision:

**Class A — Real-time log (259 ads, exact date):** The status-change logger caught these transitions live from the TikTok API. All occurred before election day:

| Date | Day | Accounts hit | Ads removed |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2026-05-18 | Mon | 1 | **1** |
| 2026-05-20 | Wed | 6 | **49** ← pre-election sweep |
| 2026-05-21 | Thu | 16 | **209** ← pre-election sweep |

**Class B — "Retro" detections (372 ads, removal date uncertain but pre-election):** These ads were found with `status_statement = "Removed from TikTok"` on May 26 (first batch `/ad/detail/` run). However, they were already absent from the TikTok API search results well before that — confirming the enforcement happened before our status-check run, not as a post-election retroactive sweep.

| Group | Confirmed absent from API by | Last seen active | Ads |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A — well before election week | May 17–18 | Sep 2025 – Mar 2026 | **143** |
| B — during election week, pre-vote | May 19–21 | Mar 22 – May 9, 2026 | **224** |
| C — around polling day | May 22–24 | May 21–22, 2026 | **4** |
| D — post-election | May 25–26 | May 23, 2026 | **1** |

**Bottom line:** 630 of 631 removals occurred before or on election day. This was not a retroactive post-election sweep — enforcement was ongoing throughout the campaign.

## 11. Key Findings


- **884–885 political ads** tracked from the TikTok Commercial Content Library for Cyprus 2026.
- **631 ads (71%)** were removed by TikTok for violating terms — the highest enforcement rate of any comparable campaign tracked in this pipeline.
- **Enforcement was pre-election, not retroactive**: 630 of 631 removals were confirmed before or on election day (May 24). The monitoring pipeline confirmed that 367 of the 372 "retro" removals were already absent from TikTok's API before the election, ruling out a post-election sweep.
- **Two confirmed mass-enforcement sweeps**: May 20 (49 ads, 6 accounts) and May 21 (209 ads, 15 accounts), both pre-election. An additional 143 ads were gone before election week even began (last seen Sep 2025 – Mar 2026), and 224 more were absent by May 21 (last seen through May 9).
- **`@theodosisavgousti`** lost 97 ads in a single day (May 21) — the largest single-account enforcement action in the dataset.
- **12 ads were still active on election day** (May 24), all organic — zero enforcement removals reached the ballot.
- **`@adiafthoroi`** (40 ads, ~3.84M impressions upper, €10,592 spend mid) remains unmatched to any known candidate or party — largest unidentified advertiser in the dataset.
- **Peak active day**: May 19 (Tue) — 59 ads simultaneously running.
- **Biggest wind-down day**: May 20 (Wed) — 69 ads last confirmed live, coinciding with the first enforcement sweep.
- **Post-election (May 25)**: 83 additional ads disappeared organically from the API — campaigns self-withdrawing the day after results.
